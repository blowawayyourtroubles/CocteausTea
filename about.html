The Origins of Cocteau's Tea

In 1963, a mysterious Frenchman named Lucien Leclair opened Cocteau's Tea in Paris, claiming inspiration from Jean Cocteau himself. Rumors swirled—some said Lucien was an artist's muse, others whispered he was a character brought to life by Cocteau's imagination. The enigmatic tea house quickly gained a reputation for blends so luxurious and complex they seemed almost otherworldly.

Yves Saint Laurent was a frequent visitor, retreating to a velvet-draped room for secretive tastings. Then, on the night of a grand masquerade in 1974, Lucien vanished without a trace.

"Years later, a new proprietor known only as M.C. reopened the shop, preserving its haunting elegance. Is it a portal to dreams or an elaborate ruse? At Cocteau's Tea, the line between reality and imagination is deliciously blurred."

Timeline of the Enigma

1963

Shop founded by Lucien Cocteau, inspired by the enigmatic artist Jean Cocteau. The tea house quickly became a cultural hub in Paris.

1972

Lucien mysteriously disappears during a grand masquerade, leaving behind unanswered questions and a legacy of intrigue.

2001

M.C. reopens the shop, preserving its haunting elegance while introducing new blends and modern rituals.

2025

Cocteau's Tea continues to enchant visitors with its timeless charm, blending history, mystery, and exquisite tea experiences.

Crafting Timeless Elegance

At Cocteau's Tea, we believe that every sip is an invitation to indulge in artful moments. Our small-batch teas are meticulously blended from the finest, sustainably sourced leaves and botanicals.

Our bespoke steepware, crafted with lead-free materials, transforms each brewing ritual into an immersive experience. Whether you're lost in reverie or hosting an intimate gathering, we infuse everyday life with poetic elegance.

The Original Shop

40 rue du Faubourg National, Paris

Nestled in the heart of the Marais district, the shop is tucked away behind an unassuming façade, marked only by a brass plaque engraved with a single teacup. Step through the heavy oak door, and you're greeted by an ambiance that feels like stepping into a dream—velvet drapes, flickering candelabras, and the faint aroma of bergamot and jasmine.

The location itself is steeped in mystery; locals whisper that the building once housed secret salons frequented by surrealists and poets. Some say the walls hold echoes of whispered verses, making every visit an experience that transcends time.
